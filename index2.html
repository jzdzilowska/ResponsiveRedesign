Responsive Redesign:
Politechnika Krakowska

1. Overview

Responsive design ensures optimal viewing across all devices. Politechnika Krakowska's website, despite recent updates, still lacks the aforementioned, resulting in a frustrating user experience. As a former applicant to their architecture program, I experienced the aforementioned firsthand. My redesign focuses on transforming the site into an intuitive, visually engaging platform worthy of a leading design university.

2. Problems Identified

The Politechnika Krakowska website serves as a critical hub for prospective students, researchers, and staff, yet its current design fails to meet modern usability and accessibility standards. Despite its architectural focus, the site lacks intuitive navigation and visual cohesion, creating unnecessary friction for users. Key Issues Identified Description
Navigation & Efficiency
  • Overwhelming Dropdown Menus: The submenus are cluttered with unrelated items, increasing cognitive load.
  • Redundant Elements: Multiple navbars (5 of them!) and the unnecessary accessibility toolbar (with unlabeled and adding-confusion options easily accessible via built-in tools) add confusion.
  • Poor Visibility of Critical Features: Important elements blend into sponsor logos.
  • Poor Navbar Positioning: Users must scroll up to access menus.
Learnability
  • Inconsistent Design Language: Disjointed color schemes hinder navigation.
  • Overloaded Homepage: Too much text and visuals bury key actions.
  • Unclear CTAs: Buttons lack visual priority.
Memorability
  • Generic Aesthetic: The text-heavy design fails to leave an impression.
  • Poor Spatial Awareness: Users struggle to retrace steps with the amount of inconsistent visual cues.
Accessibility
  • Low Contrast Ratios: Most text fails WCAG standards. Low contrast between text and background colors, with text sharing similar shades of blue.
  • Unlabeled Interactive Elements: Icons lack alt text and/or labels.
  • Non-Responsive Components: Critical content is cut off on narrow screens and viewports different than desktop.

3. Visual Redesign

My redesign focuses on enhancing the site's visual appeal and usability, featuring a clean, modern aesthetic with a focus on intuitive navigation and accessibility.

Prior to addressing all of the issues mentioned within the problems section and ensuring responsiveness accross all screen sizes, I first crafted a cohesive visual identity reflective of the university's architectural focus, including novel logos, custom typography, and a color palette rooted in Politechnika's heritage.
